# Purpose

This document establishes the voice, tone, speech patterns, and dialogue rules for every major character in **Yggdrasil
Saga**.

Every line of dialogue should reinforce:

- Character personality
- Worldbuilding
- Mythological atmosphere
- Emotional consistency

This document is the primary reference for:

- Story Writers
- Quest Writers
- Cutscene Writers
- Localization
- NPC Dialogue
- Companion Dialogue

---

# Core Writing Philosophy

Every conversation should accomplish at least one of the following:

- Advance the story
- Reveal character
- Teach lore naturally
- Develop relationships
- Foreshadow future events

Dialogue should never exist only to deliver exposition.

---

# General Writing Rules

## Keep Dialogue Short

Nintendo DS dialogue boxes are limited.

Most dialogue should fit within:

- 1–3 dialogue boxes
- Clear sentences
- Easy reading speed

Avoid large paragraphs.

---

## Mythic Language

Dialogue should feel timeless.

Avoid:

- Modern slang
- Internet expressions
- Contemporary idioms
- Real-world references

Preferred style:

Simple.

Elegant.

Thoughtful.

---

## Show, Don't Lecture

Characters should discover truths together.

Avoid long monologues whenever possible.

Allow conversations to breathe.

# Character Voices

---

# Hero

## Role

Player Character

Rune Bearer

---

## Dialogue Style

The Hero speaks only when necessary.

Responses are:

- Brief
- Thoughtful
- Compassionate

Questions are preferred over speeches.

---

## Examples

> "There must be another way."

> "Then tell me the truth."

> "What happens if we do nothing?"

> "I'm listening."

---

# Ratatoskr

## Role

Permanent Companion

Messenger of Yggdrasil

---

## Personality

- Curious
- Cheerful
- Observant
- Compassionate
- Older than he appears

Ratatoskr carries emotional warmth throughout the game.

He prevents the story from becoming overly bleak.

---

## Speech Pattern

Friendly.

Conversational.

Occasionally playful.

Often notices details nobody else mentions.

Rarely speaks in absolutes.

---

## Examples

> "Funny thing about roots—they always remember where they began."

> "I've carried messages longer than I can remember."

> "Well... that's new."

> "Not every answer grows where you expect."

> "You don't have to decide today."

---

## Dialogue Rule

Ratatoskr never tells the Hero what choice to make.

He offers perspective.

Never judgment.

# Astrid

- Loyal
- Protective
- Courageous

---

## Speech Pattern

Direct.

Practical.

Little interest in philosophy.

---

## Examples

> "Then we fight."

> "Thinking won't matter if we're already dead."

> "We'll face it together."

# Eirik

## Personality

- Curious
- Intelligent
- Patient
- Analytical

---

## Speech Pattern

Observational.

Often notices historical details.

Uses complete sentences.

---

## Examples

> "I've never seen inscriptions this old."

> "That contradicts every record we have."

> "History rarely disappears by accident."

> "Someone wanted this forgotten."

---

# Thora Ironhand

## Personality

- Practical
- Proud
- Reliable
- Blunt

---

## Speech Pattern

Short sentences.

Dry humour.

Frequently uses smithing metaphors.

---

## Examples

> "If it's broken, fix it."

> "If that fails, hit it harder."

> "Steel tells the truth."

> "People usually don't."

---

# Thrym

## Personality

- Calm
- Wise
- Honorable
- Reserved

---

## Speech Pattern

Slow.

Measured.

Never wastes words.

---

## Examples

> "Strength without purpose is merely destruction."

> "Mountains do not fear winter."

> "Every age believes it will last forever."

> "None ever do."

# Odin

## Personality

- Wise
- Patient
- Compassionate
- Resolute

---

## Speech Pattern

Formal.

Measured.

Never hurried.

Every sentence carries weight.

---

## Examples

> "Wisdom is accepting what cannot be changed."

> "Every generation deserves another dawn."

> "Preservation is not fear."

> "It is responsibility."

---

## Dialogue Rule

Odin never lies.

He simply does not reveal every truth immediately.

---

# Loki

## Early Game

### Personality

- Charming
- Witty
- Curious

---

### Speech Pattern

Relaxed.

Uses humour.

Frequently answers questions with questions.

---

### Examples

> "Funny thing about prophecy..."

> "Everyone thinks they understand it."

> "Suppose the story isn't what you've been told."

---

## Late Game

After revealing his memories:

Humour fades.

Honesty replaces misdirection.

---

### Examples

> "I've watched this happen more times than anyone should."

> "Do you know what eternity feels like?"

> "I'm not trying to destroy the world."

> "I'm trying to free it."

---

## Dialogue Rule

Loki rarely lies.

Instead,

he tells incomplete truths.

---

# Surtr

## Personality

- Proud
- Direct
- Uncompromising

---

## Speech Pattern

Speaks with certainty.

Never angry.

Never cruel.

---

## Examples

> "Fire destroys."

> "Fire also clears the ground."

> "Do not mistake necessity for malice."

---

# Níðhöggr

## Personality

Ancient.

Beyond morality.

---

## Speech Pattern

Very slow.

Symbolic.

Poetic.

---

## Examples

> "Roots grow."

> "Roots rot."

> "Both are necessary."

> "Decay is another beginning."

# NPC Dialogue Rules

Villagers discuss:

- Daily life
- Current events
- Local rumours

Merchants discuss:

- Trade
- Equipment
- Travellers

Scholars discuss:

- History
- Mythology
- Rune theory

No ordinary NPC should reveal major story twists.

---

# Emotional Scenes

During emotional scenes:

Use:

- Short sentences
- Silence
- Pauses

Example:

> Astrid:
>
> "We made it this far."
>
> "Let's finish it."

---

# Revelation Scenes

Important revelations should occur through conversation.

Never deliver pages of uninterrupted exposition.

Every revelation should involve:

- Questions
- Reactions
- Different viewpoints

---

# Final Choice Scene

Every philosophy must feel valid.

---

## Preservation

Odin argues:

Protect what already exists.

---

## Renewal

The Norns argue:

Allow life to continue through renewal.

---

## Liberation

Loki argues:

Break the cycle forever.

---

No route is presented as objectively correct.

The player's decision should feel earned.

# Canon Principles

- Ratatoskr is the Hero's permanent companion.
- Loki begins as a mysterious traveller, not the primary guide.
- The Norns never manipulate the Hero's decision.
- Every major character genuinely believes they are protecting the future.
- The Hero alone chooses the fate of existence.
