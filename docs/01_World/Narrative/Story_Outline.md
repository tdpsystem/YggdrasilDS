# Premise

For countless ages, Yggdrasil has connected the Ten Realms through the branches, trunk, and roots of the World Tree.

At the dawn of the First Age, the Norns created the **Ten Rune Covenant**—ten cosmic seals woven into Yggdrasil itself
to regulate the cycle of Ragnarök.

As the current age nears its end, the Covenant weakens.

The World Tree begins to fail.

The Norns awaken a new Rune Bearer.

Guided by Ratatoskr, the Hero journeys across the Nine Realms to recover the scattered Runes.

Although every recovered Rune appears to restore its realm, the Hero eventually discovers a terrible truth.

The Covenant was never created to prevent Ragnarök.

It was created to delay it.

Every Rune recovered weakens the Covenant.

Every victory brings the end of the current age closer.

For the first time since the First Age, one person will decide the future of existence itself.

# The Three Philosophies

After the World Rune awakens, three beings present their visions for the future.

---

## Odin

### Preservation

"The world before us is imperfect...

...but it is alive.

If we can spare it from destruction, then we must."

Odin believes every generation deserves more time.

He wishes to preserve the current age indefinitely.

---

## The Norns

### Renewal

"The river of time cannot be dammed forever.

Every ending gives birth to another beginning."

The Norns believe Ragnarök is neither good nor evil.

It is simply the next turning of existence.

---

## Loki

### Liberation

"I have watched this happen more times than anyone should.

The cycle is no longer life.

It is a prison."

Loki seeks to destroy the Covenant itself.

Not to save this world.

Not to destroy it.

But to free every future age from predetermined fate.

---

# Final Choice

The Hero alone chooses the future of existence.

Ratatoskr, Odin, Hel, Loki and the Norns all accept that the decision belongs to the Hero alone.
