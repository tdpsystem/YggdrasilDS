# Purpose

This document defines the mythology, cosmology, divine hierarchy, and metaphysical rules of the world of **Yggdrasil
Saga**.

It is the highest lore reference used by:

- Writers
- Quest Designers
- Dungeon Designers
- Character Writers
- Artists
- Localization
- Future Expansions

Gameplay systems should always support the mythology established here.

---

# Design Philosophy

Yggdrasil Saga draws inspiration from:

- Norse Mythology
- The Poetic Edda
- The Prose Edda
- Scandinavian Folklore

The game is **not** a historical recreation.

Instead, it presents an original fantasy interpretation built upon Norse mythological foundations.

---

# Core Themes

## Cycles

Everything follows a cycle.

- Birth
- Growth
- Decline
- Death
- Renewal

The central conflict asks whether that cycle should continue.

---

## Fate

The future exists.

It can be seen.

It can sometimes be delayed.

But whether it can truly be changed remains the central mystery.

---

## Memory

Memory survives longer than kingdoms.

The greatest burden belongs to those who remember previous ages.

---

## Choice

For countless ages every Rune Bearer unknowingly chose Preservation.

The Hero becomes the first Rune Bearer capable of making a fully informed decision.

---

# Creation of Existence

## Ginnungagap

Before existence there was only the Great Void.

Between:

- Muspelheim
- Niflheim

Fire met ice.

Life emerged.

---

## Ymir

The first giant.

The ancestor of all giantkind.

His body became the foundation of creation.

| Body   | Creation  |
|--------|-----------|
| Flesh  | Earth     |
| Blood  | Seas      |
| Bones  | Mountains |
| Skull  | Sky       |
| Breath | Wind      |

Echoes of Ymir still exist throughout the worlds.

---

# The Birth of Yggdrasil

As existence stabilized, the World Tree grew.

Yggdrasil became:

- The bridge between worlds
- The foundation of reality
- The keeper of memory
- The structure supporting fate itself

Every realm became connected through its branches, trunk, and roots.

---

# The Norns

The Norns appeared beside Yggdrasil during the First Age.

Unlike many interpretations of Norse mythology:

The Norns serve neither Odin nor Loki.

They answer only to Fate itself.

---

## Urðr

Represents:

The Past

Keeper of memory.

---

## Verðandi

Represents:

The Present

Maintains the current age.

Guardian of the Rune of Ice.

---

## Skuld

Represents:

The Future

Embodies possibility.

---

# The Ten Rune Covenant

During the First Age, the Norns created the Ten Rune Covenant.

Its purpose was never to prevent Ragnarök.

Its purpose was to regulate it.

The Covenant allows each age enough time to flourish before its inevitable conclusion.

Without the Covenant:

Reality would collapse into uncontrolled destruction.

---

| Rune     | Guardian                      | Location           |
|----------|-------------------------------|--------------------|
| Craft    | Freyr, Freyja, Heimdall       | Midgard            |

Removing them weakens it.

# Odin

King of Asgard.

Odin understands the truth of the Rune Covenant.

He believes every generation deserves more time.

His philosophy is known as:

## Preservation

He wishes to delay Ragnarök for as long as possible.

---

# Loki

Unlike every other being,

Loki remembers every previous age.

He has witnessed countless civilizations rise and disappear.

To him,

The cycle is no longer beautiful.

It is imprisonment.

His philosophy is:

## Liberation

Destroy the Covenant.

End the cycle forever.

---

# Ratatoskr

Messenger of Yggdrasil.

Ratatoskr carries messages between every cosmic power.

He serves:

- Odin
- The Norns
- Loki
- Hel

Yet belongs to none of them.

He never attempts to influence the Hero's decision.

He believes every age deserves to choose its own future.

---

# Hel

Guardian of endings.

Hel does not oppose life.

Nor does she seek death.

She simply ensures that every ending occurs with dignity.

She understands Loki better than anyone.

---

# Surtr

Guardian of the Rune of Fire.

Surtr exists to fulfill his role within Ragnarök.

He is neither evil nor cruel.

Without destruction,

Nothing new could ever grow.

---

# Ragnarök

Ragnarök is not an apocalypse.

It is transformation.

Every age eventually reaches its conclusion.

The Covenant determines when that conclusion occurs.

---

# Previous Ages

The current world is not the first.

Nor the second.

Nor the tenth.

Countless civilizations have already existed.

Each ended.

Each gave birth to another.

Only:

- Loki
- Ratatoskr (partially)
- Hel

remember fragments of those earlier worlds.

---

# The Covenant Core

At the Heart of Yggdrasil lies the Covenant Core.

It regulates:

- Fate
- Rune Resonance
- Realm Stability
- Ragnarök

Only after recovering every Rune can the Hero awaken it.

---

# The Three Philosophies

## Preservation

Representative:

Odin

Belief:

Protect the present.

---

## Renewal

Representatives:

The Norns

Belief:

Allow the cycle to continue.

---

## Liberation

Representative:

Loki

Belief:

Destroy the cycle completely.

---

# Final Truth

Every previous Rune Bearer unknowingly chose Preservation.

The Hero becomes the first to choose with complete understanding.

The future of existence has never truly been free.

Until now.

---

# Canon Principles

- The Ten Rune Covenant was created by the Norns.
- Ratatoskr is the Hero's permanent companion.
- Odin represents Preservation.
- The Norns represent Renewal.
- Loki represents Liberation.
- Surtr fulfills his role rather than seeking conquest.
- The Hero alone determines the fate of existence.
